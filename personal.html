Saul Tobias — Introduction
Saul Tobias

Born on the 13th of October, 2009 — a Libra. From an early age, I was a reclusive person, finding comfort in solitude and independence. I learned most of what I know through my own experience, forging my own path quietly.

Socializing with peers was never easy back then — I wasn't exactly a loner, but I certainly wasn't anyone's closest friend either. People often called me smart, even one of a kind, though I'd hesitate to say that still holds true.

As a child I wasn't a troublemaker, though I had my moments. I struggled with anger — a habit of suppression that would eventually boil over. What I mostly did was simple: play games and explore the internet. Technology was always my world.

Growing Up

In my early teenage years, I found myself confused about my direction. The pandemic arrived just as I was stepping into adolescence, keeping me indoors and alone — and I became even more immersed in the absence of companionship.

That time was defined by consuming media, exploring what I liked, and taking the first quiet steps toward forming a self-identity. I hadn't fully grasped who I was yet, but the searching had begun.

Life was largely uneventful — just me and my own thoughts — but I was still happy-go-lucky, blissfully unaware of much of the world's complexity. There was still so much left to learn.

Finding myself
Recent Years
8th Grade Started out quiet, but found my footing. A friend group of five made it genuinely enjoyable — the first real social ground I'd stand on.
9th Grade The true beginning. Curiosity took over — new friends, new feelings. I became interested in a familiar face from elementary school. Nothing came of it, but something shifted inside me.
10th Grade My prime. Social life was at its peak — I hung out with many people, befriended nearly everyone, and found myself in the highest social circles of my class. Grades followed suit, reaching an all-time high.
11th Grade — Now Senior High School. The search for purpose has become more conscious, more intentional. I am still finding my way — trying to discover what it is I'm meant to do, and who I am meant to become.
